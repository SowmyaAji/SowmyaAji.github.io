hi! [My bewildering journey into coding.]

It has been a long, strange journey of a dancer, theatreperson, some-time singer, hiker, typist, film technician, radio announcer, TV actress, journalist, mother, knitter, writer and now, software developer.

It has been fantastic to wend through the many curves, catching the curveballs. A lot of it has been fun, some of it has been formidable to overcome.

The latest twist in this roller-coaster ride is the one from journalist to developer. I loved being a journalist and I still am one, ocassionally. It is time for change, though. To apply my thirst for knowledge and challenges to another sector.

I thought about it and decided that it was technology that puzzled me the most. And scared me, witless. I was getting out-of-sync with the world and I had to conquer this fear.

Then I had an epiphany: it was fascinating. What is the magic behind the screen on my laptop and phone? The minute I got curious, I knew: I would tackle technology head-on.

Momentum Learning gave me the break I needed. Clinton Dreisbach, assisted by Rebecca Conley put us through the paces for three endless months and ensured that I, along with 15 of my classmates, learnt to program apps.

They taught us to know the ropes of how to learn more and more, as technology changes every day. And to love it.
